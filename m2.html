Why SEER is appropriate

SEER provides population-level cancer registry data with standardized staging, receptor status, survival follow-up, and demographic variables. The 2018+ window aligns with EOD 2018 staging and modern molecular marker availability.

  • Government-maintained registry with strong provenance.
  • Large enough for temporal validation and subgroup analysis.
  • Includes key predictors: stage, subtype, grade, age, race, income context.

Acquisition workflow

How raw SEER data becomes a project-ready source

AccessRegister for SEER Research Plus data and follow the data-use agreement.
FilterSelect breast cancer diagnoses using ICD-O-3 site C50 and years 2018-2023.
ExportUse SEER*Stat to export full-variable and common-variable CSV files.
PreserveKeep raw CSV files immutable and record file checksums.
DocumentRecord provenance, cohort scope, feature scope, and access limitations.

Clinically relevant predictors

The retained set includes age, sex, race, marital status, income, rural-urban code, tumor size, histology, grade, EOD stage, T/N/M recodes, ER/PR/HER2 status, breast subtype, lymph node variables, metastatic site indicators, and outcome audit fields.

Variables removed before analysis

Legacy staging fields, non-breast site-specific factors, fully blank retired schemas, and redundant age recodes are removed to reduce noise and multicollinearity.

Bias and risk assessment

The project documents selection bias, information bias, confounding by race and income, missingness codes, multiple primaries, class imbalance, and temporal distribution shift.

Data risk controls

Bias and feasibility are handled before modelling

Selection biasSEER represents covered U.S. registries, so the demo avoids claiming global clinical generalization.
Information biasRegistry-coded unknowns are expected in biomarkers, grade, and size fields; the project treats them explicitly.
ConfoundingRace and income may reflect both biology and access to care, so they support prediction and fairness review, not causal claims.
Temporal shiftDiagnosis year patterns and follow-up time affect observed labels, motivating temporal validation and calibration checks.
